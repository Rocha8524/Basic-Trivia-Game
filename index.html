UEFA Champions League Trivia

Brief History

Since 1956, the European Champions League competition has seen some of the most memeorable moments and legendary players that has ever featured in professional sports. It is one of the most prestigious tournaments in the world and the most prestigious club competition in European football, played by the national league champions (and, for some nations, one or more runners-up) of the strongest UEFA national associations. The competition has been won by 22 clubs, 12 of which have won it more than once. Some of the game's greatest players such as Alfredo Di Stefano, Eusebio, Cristiano Ronaldo and Lionel Messi solidified their legendary status by their breath-taking performances and winning the competition. Spanish clubs have the highest number of victories (18 wins), followed by England (13 wins) and Italy (12 wins). England has the largest number of winning teams, with five clubs having won the title.

It is a tournament that that is religiously watched by over 300 million viewers worldwide. The UEFA Champions League anthem, officially titled simply as "Champions League", was written by Tony Britten and performed by London's Royal Philharmonic Orchestra and sung by the Academy of St. Martin in the Fields. The tournament itself begins in the middle of September and ends in late May or early June. The winner of the Champions League qualifies for the following year's Champions League tournament, the UEFA Super Cup and the FIFA Club World Cup.
